My Education

Pre-Elementary School

Jonathan Law High School

For my Pre-Elementary education, I attended Jonathan Law High School in my hometown of Milford, Connecticut. While this may seem like a typo, it's true. I was one of the children that participated in the childhood development class for students at the high school, which also doubled as a daycare for parents. I did not receive a degree from this program or take classes, and I do not have many memories of it either. I'm told I would always cry when my mom left.



Elementary School

Pumpkin Delight Elementary School

I went to elementary school at Pumpkin Delight. It was only about a 5 minute walk from my house, so I made a number of memories walking to and from school with my friends. The teachers were all very caring. I don't have too many memories of the classes themselves, however I do remember playing manhunt and hide and seek in the woods after class. I also remember being accepted into the gifted and talented program in 4th grade. I did not recieve a degree from this school.



Middle School

West Shore Middle School

Middle school was the only time in my public school education where I had to take the bus, and the bus rides were often more memorable than the classes themselves. I had some memorable teachers, some for better reasons than others. My favorite class was probably history, not due to the source material, but due to the teacher. One of the most memorable moments was, like in my previous schools, not in the classroom. I learned a valuable lesson the first time a group of my close friends drifted away from me. Not in a grim way, but it was the first experience I had with people changing (and changing myself!), and that's not something that gets any different as you grow older. I dod not recieve a degree from this school.



High School

Jonathan Law High School

I returned to Jonathan Law years later to recieve my high school education. This school was also a 5 minute walk from my house, which made getting up for class a little easier. I took a bunch of classes, and stressed out about a lot of things which I learned later didn't matter at all. My most memorable class was TV Production, where I got to explore doing a daily news broadcast to the whole school. This was exciting for me since a hobby of mine was making amateur music videos and original short movies with a friend who has since made a career of it. I made lots of memories connecting with my teammates doing cross country running and track and field. I recieved a high school diploma from Law, as my school did not offer any concentrations or majors.



College

Stevens Institute of Technology

I am currently working towards my undergraduate degree at Stevens for Computer Science. Stevens is a small private university in Hoboken, New Jersey. I have taken a large number of courses here, some more interesting than others. I would say my favorite course was Computers & Society with Lee Vinsel. Despite not learning concrete coding like in other courses, I was able to really get along with the professor and open my eyes to some of the ethical issues and problems created with the digital world. I actually had some excitement to read the course assignments. My most memorable moment was winning a sorority sponsored DJ competition with a brother of mine.
